The Prince by Niccolo Machiavelli

These are my notes on The Prince. I have tried to adapt the principles outlined into modern day versions. If you want to gain a historical perspective into The Prince, go read the book.

  • Work on projects that act as your anchors of power.
  • No half-measures, finish what you started whether it be competitions, bad habits, toxic relationships. Eliminate them thoroughly.
  • Don't invest all your resources maintaining something unprofitable. Streamline and focus on what brings the most value.
  • Build alliances with underdogs to avoid strong competitors from monopolizing.
  • When entering a new space, be the disruptor.
  • A centralized system might be though to get into, but once you get in you have security. A decentralized system is easier to join, but you will deal with constant power shifts.
  • If you need to make thought decisions do them swiftly and all at once.
  • Dish out praise and rewards gradually to keep morale high.
  • Give your team the tools they need to succeed.
  • Take on big challenges and opponents. When you succeed, your reputation will rise higher.
  • Protect your interests. But don't isolate yourself.
  • Be open to advice and seek it. But make the final decision. You are in control of yourself.
  • Don't stay neutral in crucial situations. Take a stand and make decisions.
  • Fortune is indeed vital, but it is only one half of the coin. With effort and preparation it fortune can be dominated.
